About Paloma Kop

I am an experimental multimedia artist, ambient musician, and creative technologist from the Catskill Mountains of upstate New York. My practice incorporates electronic media, from analog video and synthesizers to digital systems and code, as well as various physical materials and processes. I am especially interested in feedback loops, recursion, fluid dynamics, self-organizing and emergent structures, patterns in nature, psychedelia, and embodied sensory experience.

I also practice and lead Buddhist meditation and yoga, and enjoy lingering in the forest. I value taking time and space to think and observe.

Community, collaboration, and education are very important to me. I help run scanlines.xyz, an online discussion forum for DIY audiovisual art. I lead online workshops through Polyphase Portal, a collectively run online school. I sometimes co-teach in-person workshops and attend artist residencies with Phase Shift Collective.

I'm working on starting Moon Ark Studio, a company that provides creative technology services to artists and small businesses. Get in touch to inquire about web design, still and motion graphics, video editing, and more.

I received a BFA in Visual Arts and Computer Science from Purchase College in 2014, and an MFA in Electronic Integrated Arts at Alfred University in 2020 (my thesis can be found here).
